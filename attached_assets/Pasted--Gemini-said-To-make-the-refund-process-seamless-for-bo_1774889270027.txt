To make the refund process seamless for both you and the merchants using BoxStat, you need to balance "ease of use" with "protection against mistakes."

Since you are using the Platform model, remember that when a refund happens, the money usually comes out of the merchant's Stripe balance, and you need to decide if you are giving back your 1% fee as well.

Here are the essential features to include:

1. The Core Refund Logic
Full vs. Partial Refunds: Give the merchant the ability to refund the full $100 or a specific custom amount (e.g., just $20 if a jersey was damaged but the customer kept it).

Reason Codes: A dropdown menu (e.g., "Customer Request," "Duplicate Charge," "Fraudulent") is vital. This helps with your internal analytics and helps Stripe better understand charge patterns.

Application Fee Refund: You need a toggle for "Refund BoxStat Fee?". Usually, if a merchant is refunding a customer because they messed up, you might still want to keep your 1%, but most platforms refund the fee proportionally to stay on the merchant's good side.

2. Merchant Workflow Features
Refund Status Badge: Once a refund is initiated, change the "Completed" status in your screenshot to "Refunded" or "Partially Refunded."

Inventory Integration: Since this is for jerseys/gear, clicking "Refund" should trigger a popup asking: "Add item back to inventory stock?" This saves the merchant from having to do it manually in two places.

Automated Emails: BoxStat should automatically trigger a "Your refund is being processed" email to the customer so they don't keep emailing the merchant asking where their money is.

3. Safety & Security (Crucial)
Admin Permissions: Ensure only users with "Owner" or "Manager" roles in the BoxStat dashboard can see the Refund button. You don't want a seasonal intern accidentally emptying the merchant's bank account.

The "Double-Check" Modal: Never make a refund a single click. Always show a confirmation screen:

"You are about to refund $100.00 to Ling Tan. This cannot be undone. Proceed?"

Suggested UI Layout
I'd recommend adding a vertical "three-dot" menu (⋮) or a "Refund" button on the far right of each transaction row.
