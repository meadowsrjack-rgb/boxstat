The "constant white gap" usually means your global `body` background is white, and the "missing text" is happening because `overflow-hidden` is chopping off the content that doesn't fit on the screen.

Here is the fixed code.

### The Fix Explained

1.  **Removed `overflow-hidden`**: This was the main culprit. It was cutting off your "Sign In" text because the content + padding was slightly taller than the screen height on your specific device.
2.  **Global Background Color**: I added a style to the outer container to force the background to black immediately. **Crucially**, you still need to ensure your `index.css` or `globals.css` sets `body { background-color: #000; }` to eliminate that white gap permanently during "overscroll" (when you pull the page up/down).
3.  **Adjusted Flex Layout**: I switched to `justify-between`. This is safer than using a spacer `div` because it distributes space evenly without forcing elements off-screen as aggressively.

      */}
      <style>{`
        html, body { background-color: #000000 !important; }

      {/* BACKGROUND LAYER
        - Fixed: Stick to viewport

      */}
      <div 
        className="relative z-10 w-full min-h-[100dvh] flex flex-col items-center justify-between"

        */}

### Final Checklist for "White Gaps"

If you *still* see a white gap at the very bottom, it means your `index.html` meta tag is missing the viewport-fit property. Ensure this is in your `index.html`:

```html
<meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1, maximum-scale=1, user-scalable=0, viewport-fit=cover" />
```
